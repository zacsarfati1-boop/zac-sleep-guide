Your body on
less than 6 hours.

You feel "fine." You're not. Here's what's actually happening under the hood.

  1. Reaction time drops — you feel fine but your brain is slower than a drunk driver.
  2. Testosterone drops 10–15% after just one week of poor sleep.
  3. Cortisol spikes — you're in survival mode all day, muscle breaks down.
  4. Decision-making tanks — the part of your brain that plans ahead basically switches off.
  5. Recovery stalls — HGH is released during deep sleep. No deep sleep, no repair.
  6. Appetite hormones go haywire — ghrelin spikes, you crave junk all day.

How I actually
prioritise sleep.

This is the stack and the rituals. Nothing fancy — just stuff that compounds when you do it every day.

  • Morning

    Get outside within 30–60 minutes of waking. Even 30 seconds of direct sunlight resets your circadian rhythm and tells your body it's daytime. No sunglasses.

  • Afternoon

    Get sunlight in your eyes again in the late afternoon — this signals to your body that the day is ending and primes melatonin production for later.

  • Evening

    Dim or turn off all bright lights as early as possible before bed. Overhead lights are the enemy — switch to lamps and warm bulbs.

  • Bedtime

    Nose tape on (forces nasal breathing — deeper sleep, better oxygen), white noise, fan running for airflow, room as cold as I can get it.

  • Tech

    The Eight Sleep pod tracks everything and keeps the bed at the exact temperature for deep sleep. It's the single biggest upgrade I've made.

  • Supplements

    Magnesium before bed — helps the nervous system wind down and improves sleep quality. I take a triple form (citrate / glycinate / aspartate).

  • Heat

    Sauna a few hours before bed — the drop in body temp afterwards triggers deep sleep faster. One of the most underrated tools.
